15th Anniversary

From Minecraft Wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

From May 15 to May 29, 2024, several events and announcements are planned to celebrate Minecraft's 15th anniversary.[1] Alongside other announcements, a free character creator item will be released each day, with each one referencing a year of Minecraft's history, which can be claimed in the Marketplace.[1] This page only details events that are a part of the 15th anniversary celebration; occurrences over the 15 days, such as development version releases, are not detailed.

Announcement

The event was teased on May 1, 2024, in a video on the official Minecraft YouTube channel titled: "Minecraft - Shape your world".

The event was officially announced on May 14, 2024 in another video titled: "Minecraft is turning 15 | Come celebrate with us!".

May 15

The following events occurred on day one (May 15, 2024).[2]

Discounts

Until June 22, 2024, Minecraft on consoles and desktop, and all editions and/or DLCs of Minecraft Dungeons and Minecraft Legends are discounted by 50%. Minecraft on mobile devices is discounted by 70%.[2]

  • Half of an armor stand.

Collaborations

Twitch

In collaboration with Twitch, the Purple Heart Cape and Glitch Mask are released. The Purple Heart Cape is released as a Twitch drop, which is obtained by watching 15 minutes of any Twitch Partner or Twitch Affiliate streaming Minecraft (while logged into Twitch)[3] and then redeeming a provided code at minecraft.net/redeem. The Glitch Mask is obtained by redeeming a code obtained by subscribing to a Twitch Partner or Affiliate who is livestreaming in the Minecraft category. Twitch Prime subscriptions will not provide the code. There will also be a Minecraft themed emote to earn starting May 25th through May 31st by watching 5 minutes of a Minecraft stream on Twitch. Once the emote is obtained you can earn an exclusive badge by using the emote in chat on May 28th through May 31st.[3]

  • Twitch emote and badge

Google Delight

Main article: Minecraft Google Delight

A Minecraft-themed Google Delight was released.[4] After googling "minecraft", a grass block icon appears on the bottom, which can be clicked to activate a Minecraft-like mini-game where parts of the search results page can be broken with a fist, revealing parts of a Minecraft world.

  • A mushroom island. Promotion image for the Google delight feature.

A Minecraft theme is added to Microsoft Solitaire Collection on Windows, Android, iOS, and iPadOS.[5]

Minecraft website

The minecraft.net website can now be interacted with. Players can dig and mine using the pickaxe to get rewards. There are two rewards, which consist of the special 15th anniversary wallpaper which can be downloaded, and the link leading to the Minecraft Classic website to play for free.

TikTok cosmetics

In collaboration with TikTok, the Follower's Cape is released[17] and can be obtained by participating in a TikTok LIVE stream in the Minecraft category with Game Rewards enabled. To obtain the code, participants must use the TikTok mobile app to watch for three minutes or send a message in chat containing the word "Minecraft",[18] depending on the specific LIVE stream​[more information needed]. Progress for either action can be monitored by tapping the TikTok Game Rewards logo in the top left of the stream. It's essential to watch a creator from your geographic region to initiate the timer. Once obtained, redeem the code at minecraft.net/redeem. The code can only be obtained until May 31.[19]

  • Follower's Cape

  • TikTok Game Rewards logo

A TikTok diamond helmet profile frame is available from May 18 through May 31 for your TikTok account by using "Create post" to share a video on TikTok lasting one minute or more and using the hashtag #MinecraftBadge in your post.[19]

  • TikTok diamond helmet profile frame

  • The cape and helmet side by side.

15th Anniversary Celebration event

The 15th Anniversary Celebration event occurs both online and at Alexandra Palace, London.[23] It features a large blank cube. Two sides of the cube were decorated by visitors using magnets. The magnets have a number on them to be placed on the structure to a coresponding number in a large grid. The end result is a mosaic featuring a pile of cookies, and creepers in the shape of Big Ben.
