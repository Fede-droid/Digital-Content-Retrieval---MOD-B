A location is a traversable area in Minecraft Dungeons, and can refer to missions, secret missions, sub-missions, Camp, the Tower, sub areas, or side dungeons.

List of location types Edit

Missions Edit

Main article: MCD:Mission

Missions are traversable levels that are selected and entered from the mission select map in Camp. Unlike secret missions, missions play a short cinematic upon entering that features a voiceover. Most of them are required to progress the story, and many are DLC-exclusive. Missions can be found on the mainland, in the island realms, or in other dimensions. Missions can contain sub areas, side dungeons, and scrolls that unlock secret missions.

Secret missions Edit

Main article: MCD:Secret Mission

Secret missions are traversable levels much like missions, however they must be unlocked by collecting scrolls in order to select them on the mission select map. Unlike missions, secret missions do not play any cinematics upon entering, and none of them are required to progress the story. The scrolls that are used to unlock secret missions are typically found in side dungeons, a type of optional side location found within missions.

Sub-missions Edit

Main article: MCD:Sub-mission

A sub-mission is a short traversable level that is only found in ancient hunts. Each ancient hunt will contain three distinct sub-missions. Sub-missions are the only type of location in the game that can generate hunt doors, which lead to gold rooms and ancient arenas.

Camp Edit

Main article: MCD:Camp

Camp is the main hub location in Minecraft Dungeons, and essentially acts as the game's lobby. It contains the mission select table used to access missions and secret missions, as well as containing the Nether Room, a side dungeon used to access ancient hunts. Additionally, it contains the entrance to the Tower, as well as several camp merchants that can upgrade and sell items.

Tower Edit

Main article: MCD:Tower

The Tower is a location found within Camp, and is a mission-like challenge with a varying number of floors. Said tower floors can contain ambushes, boss battles, and merchants. Upon reaching and clearing the top floor, three high-power reward items will be provided which heroes can choose one of to take back to Camp.

Sub areas Edit

Main article: MCD:Sub Area

A sub area is a required location that makes up part of their parent mission. Sub areas are always accessed through doors, and always spawn 100% of the time, unlike side dungeons. Tower floor types also fall under the category of sub area.

Side dungeons Edit

Main article: MCD:Side Dungeon

A side dungeon is a hidden optional location that can be found in missions. Like sub areas, side dungeons are always accessed through doors, however unlike sub areas, side dungeons have varying spawn chances and are completely optional. Side dungeons often contain higher tier reward chests, and some contain scrolls that can be collected to unlock secret missions. Endersent dungeons, rune rooms, and ancient arenas also fall under the category of side dungeon.

Accessibility Edit

Missions, secret missions, and the Tower can be accessed from the mission select map in Camp. Sub-missions can be accessed by starting an ancient hunt from the Nether Room in Camp or through the other dimensions tab on the mission select map. Sub areas can be accessed by entering a mission or secret mission that contains them; side dungeons can also be accessed in the same way, however due to the nature of many side dungeons having random spawn rates, it may take multiple tries to generate a mission that has a side dungeon.
