MC Championship, commonly abbreviated as MCC, is a monthly fan-organised Minecraft event developed by the Marketplace partner Noxcrew and creator Scott Major. The tournament features 10 teams of 4 players, typically large content creators playing in minigames. The event happens once every month with breaks in between seasons. MC Championship also hosts a Java Edition server known as MCC Island which sees moderate success.[1]

On May 27, 2024, the 13th day of the 15th anniversary event, it was announced that Mojang Studios will be officially hosting an MC Championship event in the summer of 2024.[2] Alongside this, a two-week long, in-game live event "inspired by both the history of MCC and 15 years of Minecraft" in Bedrock Edition will occur.

Gameplay Edit

The event starts off by placing players in the Decision Dome, the place the contestants will vote before each round. They will all be given eggs which they may throw into a central cylinder to hatch a chicken. The cylinder is divided into several segments, all correlating to a game. The segment with the most chickens in it is the chosen game. Players may receive power-ups throughout the event which they may use to direct the chickens into the segment of the game their team would like to play.[3]

Once a game is chosen, the contestants will have to compete to earn the most coins for their team. Coins are typically earned by completing the objective, surviving and eliminating contestants from other teams. Coins are multiplied the longer the event goes on, so there is an incentive to play certain games that teams excel at later into the event to maximise the amount of coins obtained.[3]

At the end after 8 games have been played, the first and second place teams by coins are placed into a grand finale game called Dodgebolt, inspired by the real life game of Dodgeball. Contestants are given a bow and arrow and must defeat the other team in a best-of-five to determine the event's winner.[3]

List of MC Championship Games
Game Type Description
Ace Race Movement Contestants must run laps through a course with various power-ups and obstacles.[4]
Battle Box PvP Teams face one another in a multi-round battle where they must fill the central area with concrete.
Big Sales at Build Mart Team Teams must work together to recreate structures block by block while obtaining materials inside a custom-made map.
Bingo but Fast Team Teams are spawned onto a regular Minecraft world and must gather items from a bingo card.[5]
Grid Runners Team Teams work together to complete rooms and practical puzzles as quickly as possible.
Hole in the Wall Movement Contestants must avoid falling off a shrinking platform or being hit by bouncy walls.
Meltdown PvP Teams fight using bows while collecting coins from crates scattered across the map. When shot, a contestant is frozen but can be thawed by their teammates. Once all contestants in a team are frozen, the team is eliminated.
Parkour Tag Movement Teams select a tagger in a multi-round game of tag where the remaining team mebers must evade another team's tagger for a set amount of time.
Parkour Warrior Movement Contestants complete various obstacle courses within the time limit to collect medals, while having the option to complete optional challenges for bonus medals.[6]
Railroad Rush Team Teams have contestants work together and create the longest railroad using gold found throughout the game.[7]
Rocket Spleef Rush Movement Contestants use an elytra to keep up with crumbling platforms while using a rocket launcher to eliminate other teams.[8]
Sands of Time Team Teams are put into a procedurally-generated dungeon where they must collect sand to fill up a timer. As long as the team has time left, they can explore vaults and collect large amounts of coins.[9]
Sky Battle PvP Teams collect items and fight on floating structures with a shrinking border.[10]
Survival Games PvP Teams are put into a battle royale and must collect items across the map while the border gradually shrinks toward the center of the map.
TGTTOSAWAF Movement Short for "To Get To The Other Side And Whack A Fan", contestants must make their way across an obstacle course while being able to hit other players into said obstacles to sabotage them.

Special events Edit

MC Championship has held several special events, also known as non-canon events. These tend to be for charities or special occasions and don't count to the official event count or contestant statistics.

List of MC Championship events
Game When Description
MC Championship Monthly Regular MC Championship, featuring a roster of well-known content creators.
MC Championship Pride Annual (Pride Month) A version of the event that raises money for The Trevor Project, an LGBTQ charity. Often features pride-themed cosmetics and a more diverse roster of contestants. Often times, stretch goals are employed to encourage donations such as unleashing unicorns in the main lobby or unlocking gimmicks for games.
MC Championship Twitch Rivals Annual (Twitch Rivals) An annual live event at TwitchCon 2023 and TwitchCon 2024, hosted at Twitch Rivals.
MCC Rising Each Season A two-time event featuring a roster of smaller content creators not featured in the regular MC Championship.
MC Championship All-Stars November 13, 2021; 2 years ago (2021-11-13) A one-time event featuring only previous winners.
MC Championship Scuffed April 1, 2023; 13 months ago (2023-04-01) A one-time April Fools event where everything was intentionally made broken, unbalanced and unfinished.
Jingle Jam Championship December 5, 2020; 3 years ago (2020-12-05) A one-time event hosted for the Yogscast's yearly charity event, Jingle Jam.
