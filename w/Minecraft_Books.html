The Official Minecraft Books are a series of guidebooks for Minecraft, written for new and inexperienced players while being high-quality enough for collectors. The books are written by various authors.

Building guidebooks Edit

Title Cover Published Author(s) Notes
Minecraft: Exploded Builds: Medieval Fortress November 8, 2016[23] Craig Jelley Contains information on how to build a medieval fortress.
Minecraft: Let's Build! Theme Park Adventure June 4, 2019[24] Stephanie Milton Provides a tutorial on how to build a theme park in Minecraft.
Minecraft: Let's Build! Land of Zombies September 24, 2019[25] Ed Jefferson Provides a tutorial on how to build things related to zombies in Minecraft.
Minecraft: Epic Bases October 6, 2020[26] Thomas McBrien Provides information on how to make structures such as airships and underwater traps, as well as complex redstone mechanisms.
Minecraft: Bite-Size Builds May 4, 2021[27] Provides information on how to make structures such as firefighter planes and submarines, complete with step-by-step instructions.
Minecraft: Amazing Bite-Size Builds May 31, 2022[28]
N/A
 Provides information on how to make structures such as jungle shrines and mineshaft trains, complete with step-by-step instructions.

Minecraft: Master Builds December 13, 2022[30] Tom Stone Provides information on builds such as underwater sculptures and space panoramas, along with interviews of the builders that created them.
Minecraft: Super Bite-Size Builds May 9, 2023[31] Thomas McBrien Provides information on builds for twenty "mini-projects".

Misc. Edit

Title Cover Published Author(s) Notes
Minecraft: Blockopedia February 24, 2015[32] Alex Wiltshire Provides information on every single block in the game up to Java Edition 1.8. This is also the last book to be published by Scholastic.
Minecraft: The Survivors' Book of Secrets August 9, 2016[33] Stephanie Milton Penned by a supposed "group of underground Minecraft experts who have been around since the days of Alpha", this book contains information and tips on things such as inventory layout, home design, defenses, and additional items such as spectral arrows and shields. This is also the first book to be published by Del Rey Books.
Minecraft: Mobestiary October 10, 2017 Alex Wiltshire Provides general information on the mobs that are in the game from an in-lore perspective, as if a person inside of Minecraft's world were writing the entries. It also has information on mobs that does not appear anywhere else.
Minecraft for Beginners July 9, 2019[34] Stephanie Milton Provides a guide for building shelter, finding food, and mobs that the player may encounter, among other things.
Minecraft: Maps October 8, 2019[35] Provides a look at fifteen biomes featured in Minecraft.
Minecraft: Blockopedia (Updated Edition) November 23, 2021[36] Craig Jelley Provides information on every single block in the game up through part 1 of Caves & Cliffs.[37]
Minecraft: Mobspotter’s Encyclopedia October 17, 2023[38] Tom Stone Penned by a supposed group of "expert characters", this book provides information on various mobs, such as their general behavior, whether they are endangered or not, as well as their habitats, food diet, and predators.
