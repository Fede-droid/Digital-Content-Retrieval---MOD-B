Improvement to Template:Info needed

This may be worth the admins' attention: I've just added support for a parameter to Template:Info needed. If a parameter is present, it will appear as a tooltip when the words "more information needed" are hovered over, and those words will have a dotted underline. The idea is that people can specify "more information needed about..." without having to refer you to the talk page. Seahen (talk) 01:36, 2 February 2015 (UTC)

It does not really needed to be posted here, as this board is to report issues that require administrator assistance, rather than for seeking administrator approval (which for the most part does not need happen, we do community approval instead on this wiki (though for something this small, approval is not really required unless you are removing used feature)). –User:KnightMiner · talk 03:49, 2 February 2015 (UTC)

Adding links to http://crafting-guide.com ?

I've been working on a project to make it easier to deal with complex recipes, and I recently finished adding all the core Minecraft recipes. The gist of the site is that you tell it what item(s) you want to make, and it will figure out all the raw ingredients you need along with step-by-step instructions for any number of whatever items you like. For example, here's how you make 16 potions of healing II and fire resistance (with extended duration).

The maintainers of many of the mods I've added (e.g., BuildCraft / Applied Energistics) have found it useful to include links to Crafting Guide in their official documentation, and I was hoping you would think it a good idea to add links from this wiki to CraftingGuide as well. I'd be happy to do the work of adding the links myself, but I'd like to make sure there are no objections first!

Thanks! Andrew Miner (talk) 20:54, 9 February 2015 (UTC)

Tutorial and mod content is not covered in main pages. Vanilla recipes are quite simple and this tool doesn't provide much of a benefit for them over just looking at the recipes on the wiki, and as for mods: any decent modpack will have NEI installed, which gives in-game help on what patterns and materials are needed to make things, so I don't see it being particularly useful for anything but the most complex multi-stage recipes. –Majr ᐸ Talk
Contribs 05:25, 18 February 2015 (UTC)
