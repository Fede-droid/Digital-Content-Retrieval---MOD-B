Changing default color of text to green

I'd like to propose changing the default text color of the wiki to green, or at least making it a preference. the Css can be found on my custom css page (until line 24) as a reference. As for why, I think it looks much better (especially with the new skin), and a little more unique to the standard blue that every one else uses. It has the additional benefit of being MC green. For anyone reading, I implore you to try it out. Consumed (Talk│Contributions) 18:08, 22 April 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]

You're free to use your custom CSS as you're already doing. But no, the default should not change to a random colour just to be more "fancy". That's a no-go for any professional wiki. - Jack McKalling (talk) 18:20, 22 April 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
So, no wiki should ever change their color?Consumed (Talk│Contributions) 18:24, 22 April 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
It can be added as you have did, using personal css. Having it as an option doesn't make sense to me. -- StizzurpXDD(talk) 18:30, 22 April 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
 Oppose – Though I'm not necessarily opposed to having a different link color in general, I think changing it to green brings a questionable visual improvement that's not worth the confusion and accessibility issues that it may cause. I would not mind more subtle changes to make the links fit better in a given theme. –⁠Sonicwave talk 18:40, 22 April 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
When I read this I first thought you meant changing the text color, aka the black font color in light mode and the white font color in dark mode. But looking at your CSS, it seems like you're referring to the link color. This is not as objectionable. However we intentionally opted to keep the blue link color for one simple reason; links generally are blue all over the internet and this makes it easy to understand and associate what's a link and what's not.
In addition, using green links with our current design is somewhat questionable. Currently, the background of the mainspace is in a blue color, both in dark and light mode. Adding a green link color on top of that introduces another color to the general text area, making it look messier. As for personal taste, to me the color you chose seems too bright and "neon"-y for the wiki. And finally, in light mode your green colors don't have sufficient contrast with the background for color blindness – and it's a bit tricky to get that right without it looking out of place.
I'd not be generally opposed to changing the link color; but there should be a really good reason for it and it should fit the overall theme. | violine1101 (talk) 18:44, 22 April 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
I wouldn't say the main namespace is blue, thats just what the text & inboxes are, which are both changed. I agree that my color is a bit too bright (I just settled on it a while ago and personal taste); im not implying it be the same color, it's just a reference. Darker green is needed for the links to be readable on light mode but it's really isnt any more/less readable than the blue, and if it's changed it will be roughly the same.Consumed (Talk│Contributions) 19:57, 22 April 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
In light mode, the background of the article content is #E6EFF4, which is a very light shade of blue. In dark mode, it's #2B2F39, which is a very dark shade of blue. | violine1101 (talk) 13:17, 23 April 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
 Neutral - My issue with this is that it's not as simple as just making the link colour green. Green would look truly horrid on the spin off namespaces, so we'd need different link colours for each spin off namespace. Spin off's have two main colours that we base their theme off, and it's not guaranteed the colours are going to fit as link colours as well as green does. It also means setting up a new spin off namespace has another step. Also, the mainspace background colour is blue, so we'd kind of need to change it to be green if didn't want the green link colour to look strange. Overall, I honestly do think the wiki would look better with green links and background, that's why I'm not opposing, but I don't support for the reasons I've given. - Harristic / Talk 18:54, 22 April 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
surprisingly green looks fine with the other namespaces, my main argument is just that it looks alot better and it's easily adaptable.Consumed (Talk│Contributions) 19:57, 22 April 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
