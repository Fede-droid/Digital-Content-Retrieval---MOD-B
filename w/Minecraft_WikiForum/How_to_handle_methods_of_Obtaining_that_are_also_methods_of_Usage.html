How to handle methods of Obtaining that are also methods of Usage

This dicussion is relevant to multiple pages, which I believe would benefit from having items (i.e. pieces of content) in their [§ Obtaining] and [§ Usage] sections switched.

  • Map – Recipes for cloning maps should be in [§ Usage], for the reader's convenience. Cloning a map does result in the player having more non-empty maps, but those recipes require the player to already have a working map. Also, cloning and zooming a map makes more sense in the context of the cartography table. Finally, if a reader reads the whole page from top to bottom, it makes more sense for them to learn how maps work, that way they can understand why someone would want to clone or zoom a map. The recipes to craft an empty map should be kept in the [§ Obtaining] section though. Also, since some users will be looking for all recipes that result in a map, they will want to see the cloning and zooming recipes in [§ Obtaining]. In order to alleviate any concerns related to this, a section link should be added right after the crafting recipes for empty maps, saying: "For other recipes, see [§ Map modification]".
  • Armor – As I already said on Talk:Armor, anvil, grindstone, and smithing table recipes for armor are modifications and do not belong in [§ Obtaining]. The modification recipes should be moved to [§ Tool modification], which should be a subsection of [§ Usage], not [§ Obtaining]. The logic is simple: those recipes result in the player having equally many or even less pieces of armor, and the armor's properties are preserved throughout those recipes, which means that the armor itself is modified and not replaced.
  • Axe, Hoe, Pickaxe, Shovel, and Sword should have their modification recipes moved to [§ Tool modification], which should be a subsection of [§ Usage], not [§ Obtaining].
  • Recipes for dyeing, such as the ones on Candle and Shulker Box do not need a modification section. The reason for this is that dyeing is simple enough that no reader would need any kind of explanation beyond just seeing the recipe. Following this logic, dyeing recipes should always go in [§ Obtaining], right next to the base recipe.
    • Unless the page has been split and the result of the dyeing is not one of items covered by the page. In this case, dyeing recipes always go in [§ Usage], or [§ Crafting] is there is a crafting subsection in [§ Usage]. Dyeing does not need its own subsection (i.e. [§ Dyeing]) unless there is already at least 1 other subsection with a name other than crafting that describes a crafting recipe. For example, if maps were dyeable, and if we split [Map] (i.e. [Dyed Map] is a separate page), then [Map § Dyeing] would have its own susection of [Map § Usage].
      • I know applying armor trims is similar to dyeing. However, I think that armor trims are complex enough that they should not go in [§ Obtaining]. Also, trimming is not a crafting recipe (i.e. it does not use a crafting grid). Also also,
I don't think there's any need for a specific "Map/Tool/Armor/whatever modification" subsection, trimming recipes should go in a Trimming subsection in Usage, cloning recipes should go in a specific Cloning subsection in usage, repair repair recipes should go in a specific Repair subsection in Usage, and so on. Overall I think any recipe that allows obtaining for the first time one of the items covered in a page should go in Obtaining. This includes upgrading recipes for Netherite armor in pages like Armor, Helmet, Chestplate etc, as it's the only way to obtain netherite armor.--Capopanzo (talk | contribs) 17:39, 7 March 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]

Let's not waste our time here Edit

Once we reach a consensus on this topic, we should add this information to the style guide. This would be at MCW:Style guide/Items, which does not already exist for some reason.
