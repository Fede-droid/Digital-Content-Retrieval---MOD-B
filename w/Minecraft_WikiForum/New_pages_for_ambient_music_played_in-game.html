New pages for ambient music played in-game

The following discussion is closed. Please do not modify it.

Individual pages for music tracks are now allowed to be created. - Harristic / Talk 19:24, 19 May 2024 (UTC)

Hey, what's up.

For a while I've been glossing all over the music of Minecraft, and one thing that bothered me is the lack of pages that discuss the music in-depth. I know that we have these piece of music already listed on the album pages, but, it is worth noting our ongoing effort for granularity, including the fact that we agreed to make separate pages for the music disc, and the fact that each piece of music has their own data value, their own history for when they were added, and composition piece. Plus, C418's Update Aquatic songs are not tied together to any album as of yet, so we don't have a page about Shuniji, Dragon Fish, or Axolotl's single release.

Now, I'm no genius when it comes to how these piece of Minecraft music are played, so I do believe it is up to people knowledgeable on the composition of these pieces to give a lot of info on the way these piece of music are played. With that said, I propose that we create pages for the individual ambient music tracks in Minecraft. An example would be this page about Sweden. -- PanchamBro (talk • contributions) 20:40, 1 May 2024 (UTC)

Discussion Edit

PanchamBro 20:40, 1 May 2024 (UTC)

 Support - I don't think it would be hard to make substantial changes out of individual tracks, they have history and data values and trivia like everything else, while having a lot of unique information to be put in the infobox, and large descriptions. I'd find them especially useful for finding out which tracks play in which biomes, it's just a lot simpler to find information when they're individual pages. One of the reasons I'd like this is that it would make the soundtrack navbox not completely useless; the navbox lists every track despite none of them having their own page, it would be useful if the tracks had their own pages. - Harristic / Talk 20:55, 1 May 2024 (UTC)
 Support - I believe that there is enough to be said about most of the tracks (description, leitmotif, trivia etc.) that warrant them dedicated pages rather than some bullet points in a trivia section of a larger page or a single case in a table that gets larger over time. Futhermore, it would be very useful for Minecraft soundtracks fans (niche community, but eh!), especially considering that the C418 Wiki isn't as comprehensive as it could be and that other composers now make up around 40% of the Minecraft soundtrack. - Zamburger (talk) 22:36, 1 May 2024 (UTC)
 Support - For a game where one of the most revered and reminisced-about parts is the music, it's a bit weird that the Wiki doesn't try to give each little track some special treatment. The given sample page + the already-existing music disc pages prove that the songs can have enough content to justify their own space in the Wiki. Also solves the dissonance between the soundtrack pages and the navboxes as pointed above, where each track is an individual hyperlink all leading to the same page mariberry-hearn! (talk) 07:40, 5 May 2024 (UTC)
 Support because the songs are very influential and important, just like other game features. --Simanelix (T|C) 17:10, 6 May 2024 (UTC)
