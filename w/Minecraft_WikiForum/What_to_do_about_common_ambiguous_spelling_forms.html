What to do about common ambiguous spelling forms

I was wondering if pages like the following are a good addition to the wiki.

→ See Trials & Tales

Proposal Edit

The typos listed above are all typos of both Trails and Tales and Tricky Trials, which I personally get mixed up all the time. The point of having these pages is simply to help users who don't know how to spell either term or who could be looking for either term. Typo disambiguation pages could help editors because there is an extension (Disambiguator) that warns the editor when they make a link to a disambiguation page, like plant.

Pages that could get this treatment:

  • Sculk vs Shulker
    • Shulk
    • Shulk Catalyst
    • Shulk Sensor
    • Calibrated Sculk Sensor
    • Shulk Shrieker
    • Shulker (could be typo for Warden)
    • Sculker (could be typo Warden or Shulker)
    • Sculker Box (probably a typo of Shulker Box, but the user might of wanted Sculk family stuff instead)
    • Sculker Shell (probably a typo of Shulker Shell, but the user might of wanted Sculk family stuff instead)

Anyways, I was wondering, is this a  good idea or a  bad idea?

--Simanelix (T|C) 21:40, 1 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]

Bad idea, especially for ambiguous redirects. Ambiguous searches failing is not an issue, and creating disambig pages for supposed and potential typos makes absolutely no sense (no way shulker could be a typo of warden, come on). Only create typo redirects if they are not ambiguous AND they show up frequently in Special:SearchDigest.--Capopanzo (talk | contribs) 05:11, 2 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
I like the ones for Trails and Tales / Tricky Trials. Absolutely needed there, as it's just a matter of swapping two letters for both words. It doesn't help that all words alliterate as well (sounds nice but confuses easily). As said above as well, I don't think it's needed for the other suggestions. But if you find other cases that really are frequently misspelled, this would be the approach to solve that, certainly. - Jack McKalling (talk) 08:10, 2 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
