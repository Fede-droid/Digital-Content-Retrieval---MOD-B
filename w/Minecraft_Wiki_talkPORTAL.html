Add a new variation template Edit

Latest comment: 29 April11 comments4 people in discussion

We propose to add a new template, Module:Variation, an implementation of which can be found at Module:User:Laxystem/BlockVariation.

Despite its naming, it can be used for more than blocks (hence why we removed the "Block" word). It will show all variations of a block, for example:

File:Log.png Log
Oak Log Spruce Log Birch Log Jungle Log Acacia Log Dark Oak Log Mangrove Log Cherry Log Crimson Stem Warped Stem

The way it'll be used is by creating a template (Template:Variation) that'll add a level-two heading and the contents passed to it as a template argument. Said contents will contain other templates, for example, Template:Variation/Log (which will have a preconfigured usage of the new module, shown above). This is to allow multiple variations to be added, for example, a variation for all logs, and a variation for the oak woodtype.

The current implementation requires some sort of asset (hence why there's a missing file above). This can be quite easily fixed, however.

Please provide feedback, especially about the styling and how this should work on mobile.

How can a mob spawn at a light level? Edit

Latest comment: 2 March4 comments2 people in discussion

Most of the wiki seems to use the phrasing "at a light level". For example, from Blaze:

  • Blazes may spawn naturally in nether fortresses in groups of 2 or 3 at a light level of 11 or less.

My problem with this is that the blaze itself does not have a light level. Spawning at a light level of 5 should be similar to spawning at a health level of 80%. The word "at" describes how the thing "is", not how to it's environment is. The word "in" should be used instead when describing the environment.

Examples:

  • "I am at the store."
    • "at" is part of my state of being, not the store.
  • "I am in the store."
    • "in" means I am truly inside the store, not just nereby it. This word is used to clarify that the store is my current environment, not just an object or thing within my enironment.
  • "It feels like it is 10 degrees in here."
    • The word "in" describes the environment, not me. If I was at a temperature of 10 degrees, I would probably die.
  • "It feels like it is 10 degrees at here."
    • That sentence is just wrong.
  • "I melted the ice at a temperature of 50 degrees."
    • The ice is the thing having 50 degrees of heat applied to it.
  • "I melted the ice in a temperature of 50 degrees."
    • This should be correct, since the air around the ice is 50 degrees, but that doesn't mean that the 50 degrees is properly being applied to the ice.
  • "He stands in the light on the stage."
    • This makes sense, since light (i.e. rays of light) can cover something a sort or environment around something.
  • "He stands at the light on the stage."
    • This implies that he's standing near the light source. If the light is spot light and he's standing at it's backside (i.e. the opposite direction from the spot light is pointing), then he is probably not in the light at all.

I hope my examples made it somewhat clear that all occurences of "at light levels" and "at a light level" on this wiki should be replaced with "in light levels" and "in a light level" respectively.
