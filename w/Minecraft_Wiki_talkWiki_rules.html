What does that mean?--207.204.181.251 21:08, 5 December 2016 (UTC)Reply[reply]

Vandalism is when a user (any user, either anonymous or with an account), creates a page or edits an existing one with vandalistic intent. However, their edit is not constructive, as it would either remove content and replace it, or delete content, or add content that was never intended to be there. This type of editing is harmful to Wikis, as it adds content that should not be there, or harmfully modifies existing content. Reasons for vandalism vary. Hope I helped you out some. Proposal Edit

There are many flaws with the standard 3RR, including the fact that you can just make 3 reverts every day and that it covers material no matter how different it is. Here's my proposal:

12. Persistent edit warring is not allowed.

1. In specific, one must not make more than 3 reverts within the span of 1 week if it involves the same or similar material or the same user. When in doubt, don't revert.
1. Users who violate this rule may be blocked from editing.
2. Reverting clear, obvious, and deliberate vandalism is an exception to this.
2. Users may still be blocked for edit warring even if they don't violate this rule.

Alternate proposal Edit

Because timing and intermediate edits in my opinion have nothing to do with content conflicts, I would like to propose something else, although similar. This proposal is weaker in when an edit may be reverted, but stronger in how many may be reverted. Also, I'm not phrasing any consequenses to a violation of this rule, as I think that should be determined on a case by case basis by the related admin(s).

12. Persistent edit warring is not allowed.

1. The same or similar content may not be reverted more than three times.
0. This excludes clear, obvious and deliberate vandalism. (added after ATCN's post below; 14:06, 18 October 2018 (UTC))
1. This includes multiple people reverting the content, unless it is an interfering admin who sets the final resolution.
2. This also includes both using the "undo" tool on one or a set of revisions, and performing a regular edit that changes the content in a way that reverts the related content changes.
3. Any unrelated intermediate edits by any user, have no effect on the amount of times the content may be reverted.
2. There is no specific time frame in which content may be reverted, although the count is reset if the content to revert changes significantly over time.
3. Report the content instead of reverting, when it has been reverted three times already.

Could use some improvements Edit

Latest comment: 6 May 202014 comments5 people in discussion

"In order to keep the Minecraft Wiki free of spam and unnecessary items, there are a few rules you must follow when editing and creating pages:"

You know, I wanted to have a whole essay here. Detailed descriptions of specific issues, thoughts on the nature of policy, things like that. I guess it might be better if I just posted specific proposals.

For the introduction:

Minecraft Wiki is an online source of information about the video game Minecraft and related subjects. The wiki can be edited by anyone and is governed by the consensus of its users, derived by argument-supported, civil collaboration between them. Minecraft Wiki has a set of rules all editors should normally follow.

Rule 2 (reworded):

Do not vandalize, spam, or otherwise edit disruptively.

Rule 3 (completely rewritten):

Keep your behavior civil and assume good faith. Do not harass, stalk, or insult anyone. Address the action, not the person behind it.

Rule 4 (rewritten; note that 4.1 is merged into it):

Avoid misleading readers and other editors. Make sure your contributions to articles are verifiable. Do not add speculation, parody content, or hoaxes to articles.

Rule 5 (rewritten; note that 5.1, 6, and 6.1 are merged into it):

Do not advertise any fan communities or servers. Articles should not contain information about or links to fan communities except in cited references.

Rule 7 (rewritten):

Do not copy content from other sources, including those that discuss Minecraft. If you believe there is a legitimate need to use some copyrighted image as a fair use file, mark it as such.

Rule 8 (reworded):

Do not link or embed external videos in articles, unless there is no other way to demonstrate the phenomenon, or the video is official. For more details, see the video policy.

Rule 9 (reworded):

The wiki is not a file-hosting service. If an uploaded file is not integrated into a useful page soon after the upload, it may be deleted without notice. Uploading images for your userspace is allowed.

Rule 10 (rewritten):

Accounts with offensive, misleading, or otherwise inappropriate usernames may be blocked indefinitely.

Rule 11 (rewritten):

Abuse of multiple accounts for block evasion or other circumvention of policy may result in these accounts being indefinitely blocked.

Rule 11.1 (rewritten):

An editor may have more than one account only if all accounts used by the editor are clearly marked.

Afterword:

Rules that disallow something for articles do not apply to your userspace. If you are not sure whether something is acceptable, please ask on the community portal.

Linked Wikipedia pages are intended to provide more background on specific issues. They may not completely apply to Minecraft Wiki.

Explanation Edit

  • Introduction: my version specifies some most fundamental items, and links to Wikipedia's IAR. Makes policy less of a dogma and more of a "we don't believe it can ever be better for the wiki to do otherwise, but in case we're wrong and you can demonstrate it, we will not stop you". Emphasizes consensus (with a link to a longer explanation to Wikipedia) as the key method of decision making. Emphasizes argument-based discussions (not "I like it"), civility, and the project's collaborative and freely editable nature.
  • Rule 2: beyond the restructuring of the concept behind rules (see the explanation for the introduction), the word "otherwise" was inserted as spam and vandalism are already forms of disruptive editing.
  • Rule 3: civility now required always and everywhere, and not just in discussions and not just "when possible". Assumption of good faith is now expected. Linking to Wikipedia's longer explanation of harassment. Replaced the reference to "ad hominem" with a single short sentence at the end.
  • Rule 4: account for unintentional misleading (see AGF above), add expectation of verifiability, remove the term "nonsense" because it's rather problematic to apply. Remove "comedy" as questionably useful, AF versions could be considered "comedy". Merge with 4.1 by requesting not to add speculation.
  • Rule 5: advertising already covered by the spam clause above; unclear how tutorials could require real (non-example) server IPs; unclear how tutorials could use links to fan communities; "and similar issues" is just weird in rules. Merged with 5.1, 6, and 6.1; note that block/deletion reasons already state "Rule 5/6", so these two are already referred to as a single entity.
  • Rule 7: since we have Mojang staff biographies, it is possible that non-MC sources can be used for articles. Change "websites" to "sources" as the underlying reason behind it is copyright, which isn't specific to online content (so a physical book on Minecraft still shouldn't be copied from). Add an explicit fair use exemption for images.
  • Rule 8: removed the reference to void, where no non-official videos can currently be found.
  • Rule 9: do not refer to "we" as this is unusual in the context of policy; expand images to all files, and articles to all pages; weaker wording on deletion; "GUI screenshots" and "general wiki-related images" are questionable as unconditionally acceptable to keep if unused.
  • Rule 10: reworded to link to a longer explanation on Wikipedia, and because it's not the username that is blocked, but the account.
  • Rule 11: more specific in terms of what abuse means and how it can be handled; add link to the Wikipedia page.
  • Rule 11.1: remove the discouragement clause as it false because it does not apply to legitimate alt accounts (most notably bot accounts).
  • Afterword: remove specific userspace exemptions in favour of a general clarification on what "article" means. I don't believe the admin noticeboard exemption process has ever been used, so I replaced its mention with a request to ask on the community portal. I don't believe the "thank you" bit below is useful on a policy page. Added an explanation of links to Wikipedia pages, especially since my proposal introduces many new such links.

Rules that are merged in could be replaced with a reference to the rules they are merged into. This will allow to maintain numbering compatibility with the previous revision.

I may change the proposed text if improvements are suggested. If so, I will specify it in this section. (Please avoid editing it.)

Add a rule banning impersonation Edit

Latest comment: 21 April5 comments3 people in discussion

Impersonation of another user without the permission of that user is illegal and disruptive. Impersonation is already implied to be disallowed by the rules, but adding an explicit rule for it might be a good idea.

Forms of impersonation:

  • (violates rule 10) Abuse of multiple accounts.
    • Essentially, impersonating multiple hypothetical "other" people.
  • (violates new policy being discussed in wiki forum) Use of generative AI, because the content is not written by the user adding it.
    • Impersonating the AI.
  • (violates rule 6) copying content from other sources.
    • Impersonating the copied sources.
  • (violates rule 3) Impersonating is not civil, and can even be a way to harass, stalk, or insult to the victim.

Also, articles in the "User" namespace are clearly not exempt from the existing (implied) ban on impersonation.

To add this rule, we could put this after rule 3:

  • Don't pretend to be someone else. Impersonation is disruptive and uncivil. It is also a form of identity fraud, and therefore illegal.
