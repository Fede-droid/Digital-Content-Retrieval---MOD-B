The wither storm or witherstorm is an enormous, advanced, wither-like mob that served as the main antagonist for the first four episodes of Season 1 of Minecraft: Story Mode.

It was created by Ivor through the use of the command block, three wither skeleton skulls, and soul sand. It was destroyed by Jesse's Gang in the conclusion of the fourth episode, "A Block and a Hard Place." It was programmed to follow Gabriel the Warrior's amulet. However, due to some mishaps, it became out of control and unstoppable, destroying everything in the way of the amulet.

How the wither storm was built.

Abilities Edit

The wither storm is a very powerful creature. It gained its extraordinary capabilities with the help of the command block. Its powers were:

  • Suction: In its first form, the wither storm is able to absorb only objects while it didn't had its tractor beam.
  • Flaming Wither Skulls: The wither storm is able to shoot wither skulls the same way the ordinary wither does. However, the wither skulls were an amplified, fiery variation of the wither skulls with a unique texture, and their explosions are bigger and leave accumulated flames behind.
  • Tractor Beam: Each individual eye of the wither storm generates a constant tractor beam that pulls chunks and entities into itself unless the eye is damaged. These tractor beams also trigger the endermen's agitated state due to them making direct eye contact with their face, and cause them to attack the wither storm.
  • Wither Sickness: The wither storm had an enhanced and more deadly variation of the wither (effect) that the ordinary wither has. The enhanced wither effect was called wither sickness. It is believed that one of the ways of being infected is being too long in the creature's tractor beam.
  • Tentacles: The wither storm has several long tentacles that it uses to attack, capture preys and to destroy blocks around to help it get to its destinations. The tight grip of these tentacles can destroy even obsidian.
  • Durabilty: Although immensely powerful and durable, the wither storm is not invincible. A point-blank blast from the formidi-bomb left the wither storm momentarily "dead". However, the wither storm cannot truly die if the command block is still intact, at which its exposed ribs conceal it back into itself and spring the wither storm back to life. Endermen are capable of injuring the wither storm by ripping off blocks of its mass from the body, and in larger numbers, they can leave a hole big enough to enter the bowels.
  • Regeneration: All the wither storm's wounds could be easily regenerated by absorbing objects and creatures.
  • Splitting: Thanks to the command block, segments severed from the wither storm can breathe new life as two progenies linked to the command block housed inside the main body. Once the command block is destroyed, its progenies die with the original.
  • Limb generation: The wither storm has the ability to grow tentacles and additional heads during its transformation while absorbing blocks and growing larger. In "A Block and a Hard Place" it is shown that the wither storm is able to grow them even inside its own body so it can protect the command block.
  • Order of the Stone's Amulet's Tracking: The wither storm is able to track the Order's amulet as long it is not in the Nether or in the the End.

Victims Edit

The list below shows the victims the wither storm has killed:

  • Magnus the Rogue (Determinant).
  • Ellegaard the Redstone Engineer (Determinant).
  • Reuben (Indirectly and Self-Sacrifice).
  • Several hostile mobs.
  • Possibly several humans (Indirectly).

Death Edit

Killed By:

  • Jesse
  • Reuben (assisted)

The wither storm was powered by the command block, so to defeat it, Jesse used an enchanted weapon (with command block powers) to destroy the command block. Reuben helped by retrieving the enchanted weapon for Jesse and bringing it to them, though endangering himself in the process. This defeated the wither storm and assured it would never reanimate back to life.

Trivia Edit

  • Inside the wither storm can be found withered citizens, some of them being named characters. They are Jens, Lydia, Otis, Owen, Schoolboy, and Reuben (human).
  • The terms "wither storm" and "witherstorm" are interchangeable; in fact, both terms were used to refer to it in Minecraft: Story Mode. The former was used in episodes 1 and 2, and the latter was used in episodes 3, 4, and 5.
  • When the wither storm's middle head mutated, its eye color changed to cyan, in contrast to its main color, purple.
  • In "Hero in Residence", several posters of the wither storm are seen throughout Beacontown.
