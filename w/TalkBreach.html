"The Breach enchantment has no practicality in singleplayer survival" Edit

Latest comment: 27 minutes ago7 comments4 people in discussion

This section fails to acknowledge that shulkers have a high armor value when closed. Granted, they will probably open up quickly, but it seems reasonable to get the first hit in with a Breach mace and then switch to a faster weapon. Wormbo (talk) 06:31, 11 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]

I'm not sure if this comment should be in the article at all, but there are practical uses if we want to include them. Breach does increased damage to closed shulkers as mentioned above, but also to mobs that spawn with armor in trial chambers when the trial omen effect is active, and of course to mobs like zombies or skeletons that randomly spawn with armor in the overworld. Rampage455 (talk) 22:32, 25 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
That's false, as Breach has no effect against the Protection enchantment, and the armor points of the armor worn by mobs in the trial chambers and randomly in the Overworld is so weak that Breach has no tangible difference in damage. This descrepency is why this line is needed. SeaOfPixels (talk) 17:58, 28 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
Shulkers exist though. Also, onimous trials do cause many mobs to spawn with armor. --MinecraftExp123(talk|contribs) 00:02, 29 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
P.S. However, I think we should keep the page as your edit, because it's okay both ways. --MinecraftExp123(talk|contribs) 00:04, 29 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
Breach has no effect against innate mob armor points, which includes closed-shell shulkers. I've detailed this and its implications on the page.SeaOfPixels (talk) 19:39, 29 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
Breach does go through innate mob armor. A netherite axe does 4 damage to a closed shulker, while a Breach IV mace does 5.52 damage (in 1.21 Pre-Release 1 where maces do 6 damage) or 6.58 damage (in 1.20.6 where maces do 7 damage). Rampage455 (talk) 20:56, 29 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
