Life goal Edit

My life goal is to have fun. When I realize I have disobeyed my morals, I become disappointed in myself and any fun I had doing so is devalued.

Morals Edit

The rights of humans take precedence over our personal preferences, because when we respect eachother, we benefit mutually.

I uphold the following human rights:

  1. To live.
  2. To have healthy vital organs.
  3. To avoid pain and minimize risk.
  4. To sense and perceive.
  5. To all feasible forms of communication.
  6. To learn and understand.
  7. To think and imagine.
  8. To have emotions, and experience the meaning and impact that emotions can bring.
  9. To transport oneself and others.
  10. To orient one's body and its muscles.
  11. To extend and modify one's body and identity as one sees fit.

The rights are listed in ascending order by how important they are to me.

A small rule that I specifically use: I try to avoid reacting to my emotions, especially anger and fear. I usually think of anger and fear as warnings of risk or identifiers or problems, rather than as direct reasons to act. I specifically have to do this because my anger usually tells me to do immature and hateful things, and my fear often clouds my thoughts and prevents me from finding a way to minimize risk.
