Dungeons sidebar images Edit

Latest comment: 13 February2 comments2 people in discussion

The purpose of the sidebar images is to add flavor to a page. However, there are often too many screenshots in a mission's progression to fit on the sidebar without overflowing on the page. Because of this, certain screenshots from the progression are hand-picked for the sidebar, enough to reach to the end of the page. Because of this, the sidebar is intentionally missing certain less important parts in the progression, which is why having all of the screenshots in the gallery is necessary. For the one you edited (https://minecraft.wiki/w/Minecraft_Dungeons:Squid_Coast?curid=125099&diff=2461971&oldid=2461091) Squid Coast, it is a special case as the sidebar images do not overflow even with the removal of the gallery section shortening the page. Squid Coast is a minority in this however, and most other major location pages have enough progression screenshots to make an additional gallery section necessary. It's understandable removing the gallery from Squid Coast for your given redundancy reasoning, however the progression gallery *needs* to remain on most other pages where the sidebar would overflow if it listed all progression screenshots without skipping any (for example Creeper Woods). SeaOfPixels (talk) 00:58, 13 February 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]

I'd say keep one or the other, but not both. A page doesn't need "flavor" it just needs relevant information. It doesn't really matter if a sidebar extends down. You can always make the text line up with the images by using CSS breaks (coded as {{-}} in wiki markup). ~Anachronist (talk) 07:20, 13 February 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
