A gentle cultural note Edit

Latest comment: 4 August 20182 comments2 people in discussion

I thought you might want to know that something you said was slightly culturally awkward. On MCT:Patroller requests you asked "Is there one way we can do this to pleasure everyone?" Used as a transitive verb, "pleasure" is a synonym for the more common "please". But in American English at least (I don't know about other English-speaking cultures), "pleasure" has a second more restrictive meaning that could be embarrassing in the wrong context. In fact, although Merriam-Webster currently says otherwise, I think the second meaning is the more common one in recent years, with the first meaning almost always rendered using "please". – Auldrick (talk · contribs) 20:13, 2 August 2018 (UTC)Reply[reply]

Thank you so much! I immediately changed it. I indeed was thinking about "to please", I don't know how this went wrong. I changed it completely so this should be better. – Jack McKalling [ ] 08:18, 4 August 2018 (UTC)Reply[reply]
