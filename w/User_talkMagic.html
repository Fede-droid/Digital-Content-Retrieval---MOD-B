Patroller right Edit

Latest comment: 30 March 20193 comments2 people in discussion

Hi, responding to your comment on Discord about the patroller right:

First of all, you are doing a wonderful job fighting vandalism and making other edits, which is greatly appreciated! The patroller right comes with the rollback feature, which allows the reverting of a user's contributions in one click without an edit summary (although an edit summary can be provided, it's not always needed). Therefore, it's important to know that you should always provide a reason (e.g. "not relevant here" or "unsourced") for a revert when the edit you are reverting is not obvious vandalism. Vandalism is defined as deliberately trying to harm the wiki. Many users make edits to the wiki which, although are not improvements and should be reverted, are not necessarily vandalism because it is not their intent to harm the wiki. Reverts of edits like this, this, and this should be explained, although the later one may have actually been an improvement and did not warrant a revert. Reverting good-faith edits without an edit summary can be confusing and discouraging to users.

You are honestly very close to being ready for the patroller right, and the bar for the right is not high at all; however, it would be nice to see more of a record of leaving a summary when reverting edits that were made with good-faith, that is, not obvious vandalism. Maybe you could keep up your good work with reverting disruptive edits with this advice in mind for a week or so? Then, as long as there are no major problems, I should be able to grant this. You might want to take a look at MCW:Patrollers which has details of what exactly the patroller right does and a quick guide to patrolling and rollbacking. Thanks again for all the improvements you make here and happy editing!--Madminecrafter12 (Talk to me | View what I've done) 15:08, 26 March 2019 (UTC)Reply[reply]

I've given you patroller! Be sure to read through MCW:Patrollers and let me know if you have any questions. Happy patrolling!--Madminecrafter12 (Talk to me | View what I've done) 22:46, 29 March 2019 (UTC)Reply[reply]
