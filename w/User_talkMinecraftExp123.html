You don't need to tell people not to edit your user page Edit

Latest comment: 15 March2 comments2 people in discussion

It is somewhat obvious to other polite editors that they should avoid editing your user page. For example, if I were to add "I like cats" to your user page, then that would imply that you like cats. I'm not sure if you do, but I do know it would be w:fraudulent for me to do that.

My point is: you can trust other users to not edit your user pages. Also, vandals can edit your user page anyways, even if you put up 10 notices telling them not to. In fact, having a message saying to not edit the page just triggers vandals and rude people to be more inclined to edit the page. I'm not sure how many such vandals and rude people there are, but it might actually be optimal to not have any notices saying to not edit the page. ----Simanelix (T|C) 12:54, 15 March 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]

Combinigng recipes and netherite upgrades Edit

Good question! There is a bit of a debate on both of these topics. You see, combining 2 swords in crafting yields you will less items than you started with, but you will also have more "sword" than you started with if their durabilities are low enough to have the durability bonus applied.
