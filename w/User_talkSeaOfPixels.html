Sharpness page Edit

Latest comment: 16 May4 comments2 people in discussion

I noticed that you have reverted my revert on Sharpness. After rethinking, I understand that your new table format is easier to understand. However, the sentence about Bedrock Edition is correct. Here is the sentence:

“

In Bedrock Edition, each level of Sharpness adds 1.25 extra damage, and always rounds down.

 „
— Sharpness

This sentence actually means the exact same thing as the sentence you wrote because:

When an item is enchanted to Sharpness I, it adds 1.25 × 0.625 extra damage, but since it's always rounded down, it becomes 1 extra damage.

When an item is enchanted to Sharpness II, it adds 2*1.25 = 2.5 × 1.25 extra damage, but as it's always rounded down, it becomes 2 extra damage.

When an item is enchanted to Sharpness III, it adds 3*1.25 = 3.75 × 1.875 extra damage, but as it's always rounded down, it becomes 3 extra damage.

When an item is enchanted to Sharpness IV, it adds 4*1.25 = 5 extra damage. This time, there is no rounding, so it stays as 5 extra damage. This creates the illusion that the forth level adds 2 attack damage. In reality, this is only because 4 times 1.25 equals 4*1 + 4*0.25 (remember to use BEDMAS), and therefore equals to 4+1, which is 5. For Sharpness III, the equation is 3 times 1.25, which is 3*1 + 3*0.25, which is 3+0.75, or 3.75. However, as the original sentence says, it is always rounded down. Therefore, the 3.75 becomes just 3, which give the illusion that it just added one level.

When an item is enchanted to Sharpness V, it adds 5*1.25 = 6.25 × 3.125 extra damage, but it's now rounded down again, making it 6 extra damage.

I hope you understood what I meant. I will change the Bedrock Edition sentence back again. However, I believe now that your tables are clearer and I'll leave them as is. --MinecraftExp123(talk|contribs) 02:39, 16 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]

P.S. The original sentence is also shorter, making it more concise and therefore a better sentence. Also, I hope this doesn't lead to an edit war and we should be able to find a solution here. --MinecraftExp123(talk|contribs) 02:42, 16 May 2024 (UTC)Reply[reply]
